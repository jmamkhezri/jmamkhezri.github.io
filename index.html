Energy & Environmental Economist

I study how energy systems shape economies, environments, and communities. My research uses nonmarket valuation and experimental methods to understand public preferences for renewable energy policies and their real-world impacts.

Research Interests

Energy Economics

Fossil fuels, renewables, electricity markets, and the economics of energy transitions. Understanding how energy policy affects market outcomes and consumer behavior.

Environmental Economics

Natural resource valuation, externalities, and environmental policy analysis. Examining the economic impacts of environmental regulations and climate policy.

Nonmarket Valuation

Choice experiments, contingent valuation, and stated preference methods. Measuring the value people place on environmental goods and renewable energy attributes.

Experimental Economics

Laboratory and field experiments examining economic behavior and preferences. Testing theories of decision-making in energy and environmental contexts.

About

I am a Kurd and take pride in my rich cultural heritage and resilience. We are the largest nation in the world without a state, yet our history, language, and traditions have endured for centuries. Learn more about the Kurdish people.

Before pursuing economics, I was a competitive martial artist, becoming the Iranian national champion in kickboxing and full-contact in 2003, 2005, and 2006. I continue to practice and have taught martial arts for three years in Albuquerque and mixed martial arts to NMSU students before the COVID-19 pandemic.

Outside of work, I enjoy fitness, spending time outdoors, and connecting with nature and animals—the wilder, the better.
