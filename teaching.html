Teaching

Courses at New Mexico State University and University of New Mexico

"I believe in making economics accessible and relevant. My teaching emphasizes real-world applications, critical thinking, and the connections between economic theory and pressing issues like energy policy, environmental sustainability, and public decision-making."

Courses at NMSU

ECON 345

Energy Economics

Economic analysis of energy markets, including fossil fuels and renewables. Topics include market structure, pricing, environmental externalities, and energy policy.

Undergraduate
ECON 457

Mathematical Economics

Application of mathematical tools to economic analysis. Covers optimization, comparative statics, and dynamic models.

Undergraduate/Graduate
ECON 405

Introductory Econometrics

Statistical methods for economic research. Topics include regression analysis, hypothesis testing, and applications to economic data.

Undergraduate
ECON 2110G / 251G

Principles of Macroeconomics

Introduction to macroeconomic concepts including GDP, inflation, unemployment, monetary and fiscal policy.
