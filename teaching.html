Teaching

Courses in economics at New Mexico State University and University of New Mexico, spanning energy economics, econometrics, and principles of economics.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe in making economics accessible and relevant to students' lives. By connecting theoretical concepts to real-world energy and environmental challenges, I help students develop critical thinking skills that extend beyond the classroom.

My teaching incorporates case studies from current events, hands-on data analysis, and discussions of policy implications. I provide diverse learning resources including videos, podcasts, presentations, and articles to supplement traditional materials and make complex economic concepts more accessible.

Courses Taught

Graduate & Upper-Level Courses

ECON 457
Mathematical Economics Graduate
New Mexico State University | Hybrid Format
Advanced mathematical methods for economic analysis including optimization, dynamic models, and game theory applications. Emphasizes analytical tools essential for graduate-level research.
ECON 405
Introductory Econometrics Upper-Level
New Mexico State University | In-Person & Synchronous Online
Fundamentals of econometric analysis including regression methods, hypothesis testing, and model specification. Students learn to analyze real economic data using statistical software.
ECON 345
Energy Economics Upper-Level
New Mexico State University | In-Person & Synchronous Online
Economic analysis of energy markets including fossil fuels, renewables, electricity regulation, and environmental policy. Covers supply and demand dynamics, market structures, and the economics of the energy transition.

Undergraduate Courses

ECON 2110G
Principles of Macroeconomics
New Mexico State University | Multiple Formats (In-Person, Online, Hybrid)
Introduction to macroeconomic concepts including GDP, inflation, unemployment, monetary and fiscal policy, and international trade. Emphasizes real-world applications and current economic events.
ECON 251G
Principles of Macroeconomics (Legacy)
New Mexico State University | Large Lecture Format
Foundational course in macroeconomic theory and policy analysis.
ECON 105
Introductory Macroeconomics
University of New Mexico & UNM Los Alamos | In-Person & Online
Survey course covering basic macroeconomic principles, economic indicators, and policy tools.
ECON 106
Introductory Microeconomics (Lab Instructor)
University of New Mexico | Lab Sections
Lab sessions supporting the main microeconomics course, focusing on problem-solving and applied analysis.
