The Role of CO₂ Conversion in Clean Energy Transition

Funder: U.S. Department of Energy
Award: $1,000,000
Duration: 07/2024 – 07/2027

This project is in collaboration with the University of Wyoming Economists, Sociologists, and Chemists. We investigate public responses to emerging climate technologies. As part of this project, my team and I conducted a nationwide Discrete Choice Experiment to assess U.S. public preferences for investments in clean hydrogen infrastructure as a public good. This project is motivated by the DOE's 2021 Hydrogen Shot, which aims to cut the cost of green hydrogen to $1/kg by 2031, and large federal investments in electrolysis, manufacturing R&D, and Regional Clean Hydrogen Hubs (H2Hubs).

Impact of Alternative Electric Vehicle Policy Scenarios on Human Behavior in New Mexico

Funder: New Mexico Energy, Minerals, and Natural Resources Department (EMNRD) — Energy Conservation and Management Division
Award: $118,000
Duration: 08/2023 – 07/2024

This project uses a mixed-mode discrete choice experiment to evaluate New Mexicans' preferences for electric vehicle policy scenarios. As part of this project, we examined New Mexico residents' willingness to pay (WTP) for both the state's Clean Car Rule (CCR) and Renewable Portfolio Standard (RPS), which establish mandates for the adoption of Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs) and clean energy generation. We build on prior work in three ways: (1) by accounting for state-level factors like fuel mix and job creation, which are crucial given that both CCR and RPS are state-level mandates; (2) by including all New Mexico rooftop solar owners who installed systems after 2020 as a separate sample; and (3) by jointly modeling preferences and attitudes using hybrid choice modeling to incorporate environmental and policy attitudes as latent variables. For more detail, see the working paper: Mamkhezri, J. "Ratepayer Preferences for a Clean Car Rule Under Alternative Renewable Portfolio Standards" (SSRN) ↗
